General info

Welcome to this dashboard. By analyzing publicly available information about the Airbnb's listings in Amsterdam, this dashboard supports the investigation of fraudulent Airbnb acitivy in the city of Amsterdam.

The goal of this dashboard is to aid the investigation of duplicate listings and support policy making regarding Airbnb.

The sidebar on the left contains the main menu. The 'Dashboard' shows a general overview of the data and all neighbourhoods. Furthermore, the 'Neighbourhoods' view focuses on the key statistics per neighbourhood, which can support policy making. Lastly, the 'Duplicates' view, focuses on the duplicate listings found in Amsterdam. This view enables the user to evaluate and assign duplicates. By doing this, listings that violate regulations can be identified.

Which data is shown in the neighbourhoods overview?

This view shows the key stats for each neighbourhood. The following data is shown:

  • risk indicator (coloured circle): The colour of the circle indicates the potential risk of Airbnb fraud in this neighbourhood. Red means a high risk, yellow a medium risk and green a low risk. This risk score is based on the data shown in the table.
  • # accounts: The total number of accounts (i.e. hosts).
  • # listings: The total number of listings (i.e. advertisements).
  • price/night: The average price per night.
  • % high availability: An Airbnb host can setup a calendar for their listing so that it is only available for a few days or weeks a year. Other listings are available all year round. Entire homes or apartments highly available (> 60 days) year-round for tourists, probably don't have the owner present, could be illegal, and more importantly, are displacing residents.
  • % multiple listings: Some Airbnb hosts have multiple listings. A host may list separate rooms in the same apartment, or multiple apartments or homes available in their entirity. Hosts with multiple listings are more likely to be running a business, are unlikely to be living in the property.
  • # duplicates: The number of duplicates found in this neighbourhood (with a matching score > 70%). One approach to mask fraud of the short-term rental regulations is to duplicate listings. This way it is harder to identify listings that are rented too often.
